Details About How To Apply

Nile Sons International School is a very special place to pursue quality education. Our school provides a challenging yet supportive environment where theory and practice converge; where coursework is both vast in scope and substantive in depth; where teaching skill, and embracing talents are the very cornerstones of the academic experience; and where opportunities to become immersed in prestigious undergraduate programs effecting our local neighborhood or the far reaches of the globe abound equally.
Our students are the perfect complement (and, perhaps, compliment) to our esteemed staff—they are bright, engaged, and wildly talented. Although the nature of each student's journey at our school differs, Nile Sons International remains committed to the pursuit of excellence and its command of vitality.

Our school provides a comfortable and lively atmosphere among students, staff and administrators alike, a center of innovation where forward-thinking people thrive. Equipped with shared goals of engaging academics and training, while also developing close friendships and professional relationships along the way, our students pride themselves on the mutual, dynamic process of learning from one another. A unique and genuine sense of community is the soul of our school.
As you complete our application, we invite you to learn more about our school by perusing our website, touring our campus, and attending an admission information session.

Admissions Steps

1. Complete an Application form

2. You will be notified with the ‘Assessment & Parents’ Interview Date’ through a phone call/email

3. You need to bring the registration documents needed “see below” on the interview day

4. Once the student is accepted, you will kindly pay the fees

Registration Documents

1

Original birth certificate

2

Six personal pictures 4x6

3

A copy of parent’s national ID & a photo for each parent

4

The last school certificate he/she received “stamped”

5

An approval letter from the embassy to study in our school for an Egyptian national returnee or an expatriate in Egypt
